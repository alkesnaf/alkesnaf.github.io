About Me

I grew up in St. Catherine, Jamaica, and moved to Brooklyn, New York in the 7th grade.
My first introduction toComputer Science was in my senior year of high school when I
took AP Computer Science Principles, which I loved.Coincidentally, this was around the
time of college applications and I was unsure of what to major in. I then decided to
major in Computer Science at the University at Buffalo(UB).

Throughout my years at UB, I've met many influential professors who have guided my passion
for learning. Notably, I've been working with Dr. Kenneth Joseph since my freshman summer,
where I've worked on many cool and meaningful projects that showed me how to conduct research
and introduced data science, among many other things. Dr. Ethan Blanton has also had a great
influence on me, as I was a teaching assistant for his Systems Programming and
Distributed Systems courses.The things I've learned from these professors have
greatly shaped my academic and professional career.
